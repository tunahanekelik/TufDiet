TufDiet Dietary Guidelines and Recommendations

General Principles:
- A balanced diet should consist of 45-50% carbohydrates, 25-30% protein, and 20-25% fat.
- For weight loss, reduce calorie intake by 500 kcal/day from TDEE.
- For weight gain, increase calorie intake by 500 kcal/day from TDEE.
- For maintenance and fat loss, maintain TDEE with high protein intake.
- Drink at least 2.5 liters of water daily.

Carbohydrates:
- Prioritize complex carbohydrates: whole grains, vegetables, legumes.
- Consume most carbohydrates earlier in the day.
- Limit refined sugars and processed carbohydrates.
- Recommended sources: oats, brown rice, quinoa, sweet potatoes, lentils.

Protein:
- Distribute protein intake evenly across 3-4 meals.
- High quality sources: lean chicken, turkey, fish, eggs, Greek yogurt, tofu.
- For muscle preservation, consume 1.6-2.2g of protein per kg of body weight.
- Plant-based options: lentils, chickpeas, quinoa, tempeh.

Fats:
- Focus on unsaturated fats: olive oil, avocado, nuts, seeds, fatty fish.
- Limit saturated fats to less than 10% of total calorie intake.
- Avoid trans fats completely.
- Omega-3 fatty acids support metabolism and reduce inflammation.

Meal Timing:
- Breakfast should include complex carbohydrates for sustained energy.
- Lunch should be the largest meal with balanced macros.
- Dinner should be lighter with emphasis on protein and vegetables.
- Avoid eating 2-3 hours before bedtime.

Weight Loss Tips:
- High protein intake (30% of calories) reduces hunger and preserves muscle.
- Pair protein with fiber-rich vegetables for satiety.
- Avoid liquid calories from sodas and sweetened beverages.
- Track food intake consistently for best results.

Weight Gain Tips:
- Include calorie-dense foods: nuts, nut butters, dried fruits, whole milk.
- Eat 5-6 smaller meals throughout the day.
- Focus on strength training to convert surplus calories to muscle.
- Add healthy oils to meals for additional calories.
