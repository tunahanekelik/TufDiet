TufDiet Hydration Guidelines

Importance of Hydration:
- Water is essential for metabolism, digestion, and nutrient absorption.
- Proper hydration improves exercise performance and cognitive function.
- Dehydration can be mistaken for hunger, leading to overeating.
- Water helps flush toxins and supports kidney function.

Daily Water Intake Recommendations:
- General recommendation: 2.5-3.5 liters daily for adults.
- Active individuals need additional 0.5-1 liter per hour of exercise.
- Hot climates increase water requirements by 20-30%.
- Caffeinated beverages count partially toward hydration needs.

Hydration Tips:
- Start the day with a glass of water.
- Drink water 30 minutes before meals to support digestion.
- Use a water tracking app or bottle with markings.
- Add lemon, cucumber, or mint for flavor without calories.
- Set hourly reminders to drink water.

Signs of Dehydration:
- Dark urine, headaches, fatigue, dry mouth.
- Reduced exercise performance and muscle cramps.
- Difficulty concentrating and mood changes.

Electrolytes and Hydration:
- For extended exercise over 60 minutes, consider electrolyte drinks.
- Natural sources: coconut water, banana, electrolyte tablets.
- Avoid sugary sports drinks for daily hydration.
