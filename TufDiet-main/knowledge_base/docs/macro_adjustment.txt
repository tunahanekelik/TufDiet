TufDiet Macro Adjustment Guidelines

Adjusting Macros for Weight Loss:
- Start with a 500 kcal deficit from TDEE.
- Protein: 30-40% of total calories to preserve muscle.
- Carbohydrates: 40-45% from complex sources.
- Fat: 15-20% from unsaturated sources.
- Recalculate every 5kg of weight lost.
- If progress stalls for 2+ weeks, reduce calories by 100-200 more.

Adjusting Macros for Weight Gain:
- Start with a 500 kcal surplus from TDEE.
- Protein: 25-30% of total calories for muscle synthesis.
- Carbohydrates: 45-50% for energy and recovery.
- Fat: 20-25% for hormone production.
- Increase calories by 200-300 more if weight gain is less than 0.5kg/week.

Maintaining Weight and Body Composition:
- Set calories at TDEE level.
- Protein: 35-40% to support lean mass.
- Carbohydrates: 40-45% for daily activity.
- Fat: 15-20% for health.
- Adjust based on weekly weigh-ins.

Carb Cycling Protocol:
- High carb days (training days): 50% carbs, 25% protein, 25% fat.
- Low carb days (rest days): 20% carbs, 40% protein, 40% fat.
- Useful for breaking plateaus and improving insulin sensitivity.

Reverse Dieting:
- After a diet, gradually increase calories by 50-100 per week.
- Allows metabolism to adjust without rapid fat gain.
- Continue until reaching maintenance or slight surplus.

Refeed Days:
- One day per week at maintenance or slight surplus.
- Higher carbohydrate intake to replenish glycogen.
- Psychological break from restriction.

Tracking and Adjusting:
- Track weight and measurements weekly.
- Adjust macros based on 2-week trends, not daily fluctuations.
- Consider activity level changes when adjusting.
